Breed Pomeranian
Age 2 months
Height 12 inches (male), 7 inches (female)
Weight 7 pounds (male), 3 pounds (female)
Life Expectancy 10-12 years
Living space Pomeranians are small dogs that don’t need a lot of exercise. A brisk walk or two every day plus a little running around indoors is plenty. Remember to keep your dog leashed since they are fragile and a little clueless when it comes to bigger dogs and cars.
Personality Pomeranians are proud and extroverted. They can display great intelligence and loyalty to their extended family. They are curious and like to be the center of attention. They are very friendly and playful. With poor training and a lack of obedience lessons, Pomeranians can develop aggressive tendencies and excessive barking problems. A little defensiveness is okay, but don’t let your Pomeranian become a nuisance for everyone around you. They respond well to obedience and agility training, so it’s up to you to show them the right way.
Behavior with kids Pomeranians can make great pets for families, but you should always supervise your pet with small children. The Pomeranian body is small and fragile – it won’t stand up to much rough-housing or careless handling. Generally, Pomeranians are very friendly and well suited to family life.
Protection Pomeranians are alert dogs and can be overly defensive without the proper training. Make sure you instill good habits early in life to keep the barking and aggressive posturing to a minimum. Toys and positive reinforcement can help you train your Pomeranian.
Training Pomeranians are intelligent and alert dogs. They are eager to please and often respond well to training. Excessive barking and aggressive behavior can be a problem without proper early intervention with obedience school in young Pomeranians.
Temperament & Behavior Pomeranians are active and alert dogs who crave attention. They love to be the center of everyone’s attention - don’t be surprised when they seem to want to be around you all the time! They seek approval from people around them, but they can become overprotective and defensive if poorly trained or neglected. Pomeranians exhibit a high level of intelligence and respond well to obedience and agility training. Your Pomeranian is a curious little dog, so be sure to dog-proof your home before your new guest arrives. Garbage cans, beware!
